About the Topic Selected

LGBTQ people face many struggles in society, including discrimination, violence, and lack of acceptance. They are more likely to be homeless, unemployed, and victims of crime than cisgender heterosexual people. They are also more likely to suffer from mental health problems, such as depression and anxiety. The legal rights of LGBTQ people vary around the world. In some countries, they are fully equal under the law, while in others they face discrimination and persecution. There is still much work to be done to ensure that all LGBTQ people have the same rights and opportunities as everyone else. The International Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Trans and Intersex Association (ILGA) is a global federation of more than 1,800 organizations from over 160 countries and territories campaigning for the human rights of lesbian, gay, bisexual, trans and intersex people (LGBTI). This datset is compiled from the ILGA databse collected over several years. It describes the state of sexual orientation lawas around the world including whether or not homosexuality is criminalised and various constitutaional rights accorded to the LGBTQ community. In my opinion, everyone should be able express their true selves and be accepted for it as long as it does not endanger the society.

Visualization Rhetorics: Line Chart

This chart utilises following techniques:
  • Simplification: I have aggregated the count of countries to create a steeply rising graph to display positive change
  • Ommission: I have ommitted those countries which do not specifically mention homosexuality to display the change
  • Anchoring: I have displayed the chart title in rainbow color, which represents LGBTQ diversity to influence the reader's opinion towards the positive changes.

Visualization Rhetorics: Choropleth

This chart utilises following techniques:
  • Sourcing: I have displayed the source of data on top which clearly states that my opinion is pro LGBTQ and this chart displays their legal hurdles.
  • Anchoring: I have displayed the chart title in red color, to express the negativity displayed by the graph.
  • Ommission: I Ommitted the Employement protection column as it was very balanced with over a 100 countries having them in place. Since this would conflict with the theme of the viz I ommitted it.
